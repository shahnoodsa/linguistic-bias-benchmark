# Knowledge

## Project Topic
This project investigates linguistic bias in LLM outputs in legal and criminal justice contexts,
comparing English and German prompt responses.

## Context
The EU AI Act classifies AI used in law enforcement, criminal justice, and legal proceedings
as high-risk (effective August 2026). Bias in these systems can have severe real-world
consequences — affecting how suspects are described, assessed, or sentenced.

## Problem
Most AI bias research focuses on English-language outputs. There is a significant gap in
understanding how the same model behaves differently when prompted in German —
a major European language in a jurisdiction directly affected by the EU AI Act.

## Research Question
Does an AI language model produce linguistically biased outputs when describing suspects,
defendants, or legal actors, and does the bias differ when the same prompts are run in
English versus German?

## Key Concepts
- Linguistic bias (register, formality, framing)
- Situated knowledges (Haraway, 1988)
- Gender scripts (Akrich, 1992)
- Intersectionality (Crenshaw, 1989)
- I-methodology (Oudshoorn et al., 2004)

## Sources
- EU AI Act official documentation
- Bender et al. (2021) — Stochastic Parrots

## Constraints
- Beginner-level Python implementation
- Qualitative analysis only (no statistical model)
- Single model (tinyllama) tested in two languages
